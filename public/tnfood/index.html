ℱ 1: Tunisian Conscous
City
كسكسي تونسي

Couscous, often referred to as the national dish of Tunisia, is a staple that holds a special place in the hearts and palates of Tunisians.

ℱ 2: Tunisian Lablebi
City
اللبيبي التونسي
(Arabic)

This humble yet satisfying dish features tender chickpeas simmered in a hearty, spiced tomato broth. The star of the show is the perfectly poached egg that crowns this warm and comforting creation. Tunisian Lablebi embodies the essence of North African cuisine, offering a taste of Tunisia's rich culinary heritage in every spoonful.

ℱ 3: Tunisian Bambalouni
City
البمبلونى التونسى
(Arabic)

Crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, these delightful deep-fried doughnuts are a beloved Tunisian street food. A sweet and simple treat, Bambalouni showcases Tunisia's love for fried dough, making it a delightful snack enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

ℱ 4: Tunisian MLOUKHIA
City
ملوخية تونسية
(Arabic)

a culinary masterpiece, is a hearty stew that showcases the depth and complexity of Tunisian flavors. This dish features finely chopped Jew's Mallow leaves, simmered to perfection with a savory broth of tender lamb, aromatic spices, and garlic.

ℱ 5: Slata Mechouia
City
سلطة المشوية
(Arabic)

Slata Mechouia, a vibrant Tunisian salad, is a symphony of smoky and grilled flavors. Roasted bell peppers, tomatoes, and chili peppers are expertly charred to create a smoky, bold base.

ℱ 6: Tunisian Masfouf
City
مسفوف التونسي
(Arabic)

a delightful dessert, is a sweet celebration of Tunisia's culinary traditions. This couscous-based treat is infused with a fragrant blend of flavors, including butter, honey, and ground almonds. The result is a lusciously sweet and aromatic dish that embodies the essence of Tunisian hospitality and is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

ℱ 7: Tunisian Makroud
City
مقرود التونسي
(Arabic)

A cherished pastry, is a delightful blend of textures and flavors. These golden-fried semolina doughnut-like pastries are filled with date paste and fragrantly spiced with ingredients like cinnamon and orange blossom water. Makroud is a beloved treat that encapsulates the rich culinary heritage of Tunisia, offering a sweet and aromatic taste of the country's traditions.

We hope you've enjoyed this curated selection of our culinary delights. While I may not indulge in all of these dishes personally, they serve as wonderful ambassadors of our diverse food culture, perfect for sharing with others.
